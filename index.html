What we believe: 12 themes
  • New degrees. The majority of college students will be "non-traditional" and require new models of education.
  • International. The number of international students studying in the US will increase by 3x over the next decade.
  • Be the best. For institutions, it is better to be the best at something narrow than it is to be mediocre at everything.
  • Try before you buy. Students will increasingly look to try free classes before making an enrollment decision.
  • New funding models. Programs with better career outcomes will be able to offer better loan support and financial aid.
  • Employers. Employer partnerships are key for graduates’ success in the job market.
  • Data. Outcome improvement starts with outcome tracking and academic dashboards.
  • What you know. The focus of future programs will be on what students can do, not how long they studied.
  • Social learning. Learning from classmates and near-peers is as important as learning from teachers.
  • Learning by doing. Many key skills require practice. This means real-world projects and student portfolios.
  • Retention. No silver bullet for student success. There are hundreds of friction points, and each needs its own solution.
  • Innovate or die. This time is different; universities that can’t adapt will go out of business.
